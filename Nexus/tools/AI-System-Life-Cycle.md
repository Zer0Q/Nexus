# AI System Life Cycle

ISO/IEC 22989:2022 defines a comprehensive AI System Life Cycle Model for establishing accountability across all stages.

## Lifecycle Stages
1. **Inception** -- define objectives, assess feasibility, identify potential misuse
2. **Design** -- architecture, data requirements, risk assessment
3. **Data Acquisition** -- collect, validate, ensure representativeness and legal compliance
4. **Training** -- model development with bias detection and quality gates
5. **Evaluation** -- verification and validation against requirements
6. **Deployment & Monitoring** -- continuous tracking under production conditions
7. **Retirement** -- when performance degrades or ethical risks exceed thresholds

## Key Differences from Traditional SDLC
- AI systems are not "set it and forget it" — continuous monitoring is mandatory
- Retirement triggers: performance degradation OR ethical risk thresholds (not just hardware/OS EOL)
- Logic is learned from data, not explicitly coded
