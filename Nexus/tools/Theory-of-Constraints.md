# Theory of Constraints

## Definition
Management philosophy by Eliyahu Goldratt: any manageable system is limited by a small number of constraints (usually one). System improvement comes from identifying and optimizing around the bottleneck, not from optimizing everything simultaneously.

## Why It Matters
Provides a systematic approach to improvement that avoids wasted effort on non-critical areas. Applicable to manufacturing, software development, project management, and any flow-based system.

## Key Ideas
- Five-step improvement cycle (POOGI): Identify -> Exploit -> Subordinate -> Elevate -> Repeat
- Constraint is the system's weakest link — it determines overall throughput
- Non-constraint resources should subordinate to the constraint's pace
- Once a constraint is broken, it moves — improvement is inherently iterative
- Policy constraints (rules, metrics) are often harder to fix than physical ones

## Tradeoffs
- Requires discipline to subordinate non-bottleneck resources (cultural resistance)
- Inertia: rules created for old constraints become new policy constraints
- Single-point focus can blind teams to emerging secondary constraints
