# Comunidades de Práctica

## Summary
Overview of Communities of Practice (CoP): groups of people sharing a common field of knowledge who learn through shared practice. Covers core characteristics (shared leadership, domain of knowledge, practice, community feeling), virtual CoPs, and Wenger's seven design principles for building effective communities.

## Core Concepts
- [[Communities-of-Practice]] -- groups co-constructing knowledge through shared practice
- [[Shared-Leadership]] -- homogeneous participants, no top-down hierarchy
- [[Virtual-Communities-of-Practice]] -- digital CoPs transcending geography
- [[CoP-Design-Principles]] -- Wenger's 7 principles for building CoPs
- [[Legitimate-Peripheral-Participation]] -- newcomers learning from periphery to core
- [[Tacit-Knowledge]] -- knowledge shared through practice, not documentation

## Key Insights
- Xerox Palo Alto repair technicians created an accidental CoP by sharing fix experiences during breaks instead of using manuals
- Reciprocity is key: members who only take without contributing are peripheral, not active
- Virtual CoPs don't require synchronous participation — written records persist across time zones
- Wenger's 7 principles: ease of evolution, permeability, participation levels, public/private spaces, value, closeness/emotion, rhythm
