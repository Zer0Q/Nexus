# I Replaced My Entire Research Stack With Obsidian and Claude

## Summary
Author ran five disconnected tools (Notion, Readwise, X bookmarks, browser bookmarks, ChatGPT) for research over two years, then consolidated everything into a single Obsidian vault with Claude as the intelligence layer. The system uses a six-folder structure, Telegram bot for capture, weekly AI synthesis, and structured source templates. Result: fewer tools, lower cost, higher-quality research output.

## Key Insights
- The problem with multi-tool stacks is not individual tool quality but the *connection cost* between them
- A single search + single context window beats five best-in-class tools that don't talk to each other
- Weekly AI synthesis surfaces non-obvious connections that manual review misses
- Structured source templates are the enabler for AI reasoning -- inconsistent formatting breaks automation
- The Telegram bot is the most critical infrastructure piece: it removes all excuses for not capturing
- System took one weekend to build; the five tools it replaced took two years to accumulate
