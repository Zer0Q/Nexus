# Tacit Knowledge as Competitive Moat

## Summary
Berkeley CMR article arguing that tacit knowledge (expert judgment, heuristics, situational awareness) is the real differentiator in the age of agentic AI. Five actions: map where tacit knowledge lives, codify it without flattening, build a semantic layer (knowledge graphs), enable human-AI collaboration, and ensure business leaders shape AI application. Companies encoding tacit knowledge into AI gain compound advantage.

## Core Concepts
- [[Tacit-Knowledge]] -- expert judgment embedded in experience, not documentation
- [[Knowledge-Graph-as-Semantic-Layer]] -- structured relationships enabling AI reasoning
- [[Codify-without-Flattening]] -- preserve decision context, not just rules
- [[Human-AI-Orchestration]] -- fused roles blending judgment with machine insight
- [[Knowledge-Mapping]] -- identifying where expertise resides and flows

## Key Insights
- Data and models are table stakes; tacit knowledge is the moat
- Mapping reveals "invisible experts" -- people colleagues rely on informally
- Codification must preserve decision context, not just extract rules
- Knowledge graphs enable AI to reason with contextual understanding
- Companies with semantic layers roll out AI use cases in weeks, not months
- Baby Boomer retirements add urgency -- tacit knowledge is leaving with people
- "Fusion skills" (knowledge curation, model interpretation, orchestration) are emerging roles

## Open Questions
