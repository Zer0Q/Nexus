# Industrial Automation and Alarm Management

## Overview
Standards, concepts, and practices for alarm management and HMI design in process industries — ISA-18.2, ISA-101, and the lifecycle approach to operator support systems.

## Core Concepts
- [[Alarm-Management]] -- systematic transformation of alarm systems into operator support tools
- [[Alarm-Management-Lifecycle]] -- continuous lifecycle: philosophy through audit
- [[Alarm-Rationalization]] -- reviewing and justifying alarms against defined criteria
- [[Alarm-Philosophy]] -- documented framework guiding all alarm decisions
- [[Alarm-Classification]] -- grouping alarms by common management requirements
- [[Highly-Managed-Alarm]] -- alarms requiring extra administration (safety, environmental, quality)
- [[Alarm-Monitoring-and-Assessment]] -- ongoing tracking of alarm performance metrics
- [[Dynamic-Alarming]] -- alarm settings that adjust to real-time conditions
- [[Advanced-Alarm-Methods]] -- predictive alarming, adaptive systems, ML integration
- [[Management-of-Change]] -- formal process for reviewing and approving alarm modifications

## HMI Design
- [[High-Performance-HMI]] -- standardized HMI: grayscale base, color for abnormal, information over data
- [[Grayscale-HMI-Design]] -- color reserved exclusively for abnormal conditions (48% improvement)
- [[Display-Hierarchy]] -- four-level structure: awareness, overview, detail, diagnostics
- [[Operator-Situational-Awareness]] -- perception, comprehension, projection of process state
- [[Information-over-Data]] -- contextual meaning alongside raw values

## Standards
- [[ISA-18-2-Standard]] -- authoritative alarm management standard (IEC 62682 equivalent)
- [[ISA-101-Standard]] -- HMI lifecycle standard (ANSI/ISA-101.01-2015)
