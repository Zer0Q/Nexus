# World Bible Business Workflow

## Summary
The article frames a World Bible as a structured, internally consistent deliverable that game studios, TTRPG publishers, interactive fiction teams, film developers, and authors already pay for. Its proposed workflow uses source-material extraction into a graph, GraphRAG-style querying for coherence, and agent societies to turn lore into an explorable, inhabitable universe.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/World-Bible]] -- a structured canon document covering characters, geography, factions, timeline, rules, and visual direction.
- [[concepts/Knowledge-Graph]] -- the world model starts as entities, relationships, constraints, events, and gaps extracted from source material.
- [[concepts/Agentic-GraphRAG]] -- graph-aware retrieval answers causal and political questions about the fictional world instead of keyword matching lore fragments.
- [[concepts/Agent-Society]] -- characters become agents with goals, relationships, secrets, and interaction protocols rather than static NPC scripts.
- [[concepts/Graph-Knowledge-Layer]] -- the graph becomes the reusable substrate for reports, queries, client demos, and future expansions.

## Key Insights
- The client buys consistency and team usability, not the fact that AI helped produce the document.
- Gap analysis is a high-value output because it exposes contradictions and missing rules before production begins.
- Graphify-like extraction, GraphRAG reasoning, and CAMEL-style agent societies form a three-layer world engine: map, understand, inhabit.
- The strongest portfolio is a complete public example showing the graph visualization, markdown World Bible, and agent society spec.
- Human judgment remains the differentiator: originality, believable politics, character motivation, and creative decisions still need direction.
