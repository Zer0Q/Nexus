# Claude + Obsidian = the $0 moat your competitors can't clone

## Summary

The vault you build becomes a training set about yourself that competitors cannot copy, scrape, or out-prompt. After 60 days the vault becomes a high-resolution map of your own cognition. The 3-layer architecture: Layer 1 (Capture) for raw inflow with zero friction, Layer 2 (Memory/Obsidian) for structured markdown a model can reason across, Layer 3 (Intelligence/Claude) for scheduled synthesis. Key decision: organize by type (observations, quotes, questions, patterns, decisions) not by topic — connections that change how you think live across topics. The daily loop: capture all day, process at 2 AM nightly, synthesize at 6 AM, weekly deep sweep on Sunday.

## Core Concepts

- Personal-Corpus-Moat — Accumulated personal context that cannot be replicated by competitors
- Three-Layer-Knowledge-Architecture — Capture (raw inflow), Memory (Obsidian), Intelligence (Claude)
- Type-Based-Organization — File notes by type not topic to enable cross-domain reasoning
- Daily-Synthesis-Loop — Capture → Process → Synthesize → Surface, runs on schedule
- [[concepts/CLAUDE-MD-as-Context-Layer]] — One file that quietly runs everything; tells Claude who you are and what you care about

## Key Insights

- Models are commoditizing; skills are commoditizing; prompts are commoditizing — personal corpus is not
- A prompt against a stranger's vault produces generic output; against 8 months of your notes it reads like it came from inside your head
- The compounding timeline: Day 1 (useless), Week 1 (interesting), Month 1 (useful), Month 3 (insightful), Month 6 (unreplicable)
- Inconsistent capture is the one thing that breaks the system — the vault degrades within two weeks
- The Telegram bot is the single most important piece of infrastructure for capture

## Open Questions

- How does type-based organization handle notes that span multiple types?
- What is the optimal frequency for weekly synthesis vs daily briefings?
- How do you handle contradictory information accumulated over time?
