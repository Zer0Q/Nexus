# 30-Minute Obsidian Retrieval System

## Summary
A retrieval-first Obsidian system built around three properties (permanent, linkable, queryable) and five workflows (capture, nightly processing, decision feeder, writing activator, output generator). The key metric is contribution rate -- how often a captured note ends up in actual output.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/Retrieval-First-Organization]] -- design your notes system from retrieval backward, not from capture forward
- [[concepts/Note-Contribution-Rate]] -- measure of how often captured notes actually feed decisions, output, or action
- [[workflows/Nightly-Processing]] -- end-of-day 10-minute pass to triage captures into structured notes
- [[workflows/Decision-Feeder]] -- query vault for relevant past notes when stuck on a decision
- [[workflows/Writing-Activator]] -- surface relevant notes before starting any writing task
- [[concepts/Output-Generator-Workflow]] -- synthesize months of captures into finished pieces
- [[concepts/Zero-Friction-Capture]] -- capture in under 7 seconds; no folder decisions, no tags
- [[concepts/Weekly-Review]] -- 20-minute weekly habit that makes knowledge compound

## Key Insights
- Most note apps solve capture but not retrieval; the vault should be designed backward from retrieval
- Contribution rate > note count; 400 useful notes beat 8,000 where 7,800 are never opened again
- Claude MCP connection turns vault from archive into thinking partner
- Skip the weekly review twice in a row and the system silently dies
