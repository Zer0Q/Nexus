# Broken Process In, Broken AI Out

## Summary
AI amplifies whatever process it sits on — it doesn't fix weak processes. Bad data is a symptom of broken upstream processes, not the root cause. Lean principles (visual management, Poka-yoke, Gemba) are more effective than training for data quality. AI transformation sequence: people first, process second, technology third. AI's first role should be visual management — making process problems visible before automating them.

- [[concepts/Visual-Management]] -- making process and data issues impossible to ignore

## Key Insights
- CMMS example: nobody created work orders properly; visual management monitor fixed it overnight by making gaps visible
- "Garbage in, garbage out" is too shallow — the real question is why the process generates bad data
- Broken manual processes fail slowly and locally; broken automated processes fail fast and at scale
- Every "Other" selection, every blank field, every workaround spreadsheet is the process confessing exactly where it breaks
- Training is a weak control — relies on memory and willpower under pressure
- Poka-yoke example: replaced long dropdown list with a tree organized how technicians think → correct option became faster than wrong one
- Weekly data review meetings with technicians gave them ownership and turned them from endpoints into system improvers
- AI as visual management at scale: surface missing info, report repeating exceptions, trigger alerts for process deviations
- Oxytocin (belonging) + dopamine (progress) as chemical rewards for change engagement

## Open Questions
- How do you measure "process health" before deploying AI on top of it?
- What's the minimum viable process maturity before AI automation is safe?
