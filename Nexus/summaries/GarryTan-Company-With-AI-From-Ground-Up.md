# Company with AI from the Ground Up

## Summary
The talk contrasts open-loop companies that rely on meetings, status updates, and human routing with closed-loop AI-native companies whose context is continuously captured, queried, and acted on. The practical design pattern is to make the company legible to agents from day one so product, support, sales, and engineering loops can close without layers of manual coordination.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/Open-vs-Closed-Loop]] -- open-loop organizations wait for humans to notice and route issues; closed-loop organizations feed observations back into action.
- [[concepts/Company-Brain-Architecture]] -- a shared knowledge layer lets agents answer questions about customers, operations, and product decisions.
- [[concepts/AI-Native-Engineering]] -- building the company around agent workflows changes hiring, documentation, and product cadence.
- [[concepts/Retrieval-First-Organization]] -- organizational memory must be searchable before agents can use it reliably.
- [[concepts/Multi-Agent-Org-Chart]] -- functions become agent roles and workflows rather than only human departments.

## Key Insights
- "AI as operating system" means the company state is represented in systems agents can inspect, not scattered across chats and meetings.
- Closed loops are easier for startups because they can avoid legacy process debt and build capture, retrieval, and action paths from the beginning.
- The multidisciplinary engineer gains leverage because product, design, engineering, and go-to-market work can be compressed into agent-supported workflows.
- Middle management weakens when information routing and status synthesis become automatic.
- Startups that build queryability early can move faster because every later workflow inherits better context.
