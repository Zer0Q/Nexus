# Communities of Practice Playbook

## Summary
European Commission methodology for setting up, running, and evaluating Communities of Practice. Based on the CoP Success Wheel framework with five success facets (shared vision, participation, knowledge retention, trust, inclusive communication). Distinguishes between passive push roles (amplifying, curating, convening) and proactive pull roles (investing, community building, learning facilitation).

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/Communities-of-Practice]] -- groups sharing concern/passion and learning through regular interaction
- [[concepts/CoP-Success-Wheel]] -- eight-factor framework for community success
- [[concepts/CoP-Design-Principles]] -- design principles for effective communities
- [[concepts/Knowledge-Centers]] -- formal bodies that structure and synthesize knowledge
- [[concepts/Tacit-Knowledge]] -- explicit vs. tacit knowledge management
- [[concepts/Boundary-Spanning]] -- connecting across organizational silos

## Key Insights
- Three community types: knowledge centres (formal, structured), CoPs (bridge), networks (exploratory)
- Proactive pull roles need more work than passive push roles
- Management engagement through leading by example is a key motivational force
- Co-ownership, convening, collaboration/cooperation are the three overarching capacity-building areas
- Boundary-spanning practices are crucial but currently limited in reach
