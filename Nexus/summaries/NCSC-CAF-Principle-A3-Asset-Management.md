# NCSC CAF Principle A3 — Asset Management

## Summary
NCSC CAF Principle A3 requires appropriate organisational structures, policies, processes and procedures to understand, assess and systematically manage security risks to network and information systems supporting essential functions. The "Achieved" criteria demand complete asset inventories, dependency mapping, prioritisation by importance, assigned responsibility, and lifecycle management from creation to decommissioning.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/NCSC-CAF]] -- UK National Cyber Security Centre's Cyber Assessment Framework for organisations with essential functions
- [[concepts/Asset-Inventory-Management]] -- all assets relevant to essential functions identified, inventoried, kept up-to-date, with dependencies recorded
- [[concepts/Asset-Lifecycle-Security]] -- assets managed with cybersecurity in mind from creation through decommissioning or disposal
- [[concepts/Asset-Prioritisation]] -- assets ranked by importance to essential function operation; dependencies on supporting infrastructure (power, cooling) recognised
- [[concepts/Knowledge-Succession-Planning]] -- critical knowledge not held by one or two individuals; succession plans required
- [[concepts/Data-Retention-Policy]] -- information assets not stored indefinitely without clear business need

## Key Insights
- "Not achieved" includes: incomplete inventories, unknown IT/OT dependencies, data stored with no retention policy, critical knowledge held by 1-2 people with no succession plan, inventories out of date
- "Achieved" requires: complete inventory at suitable detail level, up-to-date tracking, dependency recognition, prioritisation by importance, assigned responsibility per asset, lifecycle management
- IT/OT dependency mapping is explicitly called out — a common blind spot in critical infrastructure
- Asset management is not just IT — physical assets relevant to essential functions are included
- Knowledge as an asset: tacit knowledge held by key individuals is a risk if no succession plan exists
