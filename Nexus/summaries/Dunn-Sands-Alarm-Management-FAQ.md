# Alarm Management FAQ

## Summary
Comprehensive Q&A on alarm management covering standards, costs, benefits, and implementation. ISA-18.2 is the authoritative standard, with IEC 62682 as nearly identical international version. Alarm management is a lifecycle approach, not just software or rationalization. Typical ROI is 20x the external cost through avoided shutdowns, off-quality production, and incidents.

## Key Insights
- Alarm management is NOT just a software application or just rationalization — it's a lifecycle work process
- Regulatory requirement in US (OSHA PSM, EPA RMP, FDA CGMP, PHMSA) and Europe (Seveso directives)
- ACME Chemicals example: 3 plants, 3000 tags/site, $150-200K cost, $4.5M savings target (20x ROI)
- "Bad actor resolution" — fix most offensive alarms first without full rationalization
- Be careful of alarm management consultants — one company "rationalized critical alarms out of existence"
- Campbell Brown's vision: "right information, in the right way, at the right time for action by the Control Room Operator"
