# JaviLopen AI Medical Research ARROWHEAD Method

## Summary
JaviLopen spent a month helping a metastatic cancer patient using an AI-assisted research methodology called ARROWHEAD (adversarial model spiral). The process involves: (1) using top-tier models (ChatGPT 5 Pro, Claude Opus 4.8 MAX), (2) consolidating 100+ clinical PDFs into a single readable text via OCR, (3) running specialized prompts across multiple models simultaneously, and (4) pitting model outputs against each other in 7+ iterative rounds until convergence. The methodology is claimed to be domain-agnostic and applicable to programming, math, and any field requiring rigorous analysis.

## Key Insights
- The ARROWHEAD method works by successive adversarial refinement: each model reviews the other's output, generating improved versions until both agree the work is complete
- Full clinical history processing is critical -- skipping OCR consolidation produces unreliable outputs
- The methodology produces actionable outputs: additional tests to consider, possible treatment paths for oncologists to evaluate
- Automation frameworks (AutoGen, LangGraph, CrewAI) can orchestrate the adversarial spiral without manual iteration
- Top models cost money but the author argues public healthcare should invest heavily in this capability
## Open Questions
