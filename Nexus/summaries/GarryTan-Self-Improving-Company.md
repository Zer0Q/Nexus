# Self-Improving Company with AI

## Summary
The talk argues that companies should stop treating AI as isolated copilots and instead expose their domain knowledge, workflows, and operating manual to agents that can improve the company recursively. The durable asset is not ephemeral software but legible context: the company's rules, decisions, playbooks, and customer feedback encoded so agents can query, update, and act on them.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/Company-Brain-Architecture]] -- the organization becomes queryable when its knowledge, playbooks, and decisions are externalized.
- [[concepts/Self-Improvement-Loop]] -- recurring AI workflows improve prompts, processes, docs, products, and support based on feedback.
- [[concepts/Context-Substrate]] -- company knowledge becomes the shared substrate agents need to coordinate work.
- [[concepts/AI-Amplifies-Process]] -- AI accelerates whatever process already exists, so broken or undocumented processes get amplified too.
- [[concepts/Human-Comparative-Advantage]] -- humans remain crucial for taste, judgment, strategy, and deciding what should exist.

## Key Insights
- The wrong mental model is "copilots for every worker"; the stronger model is an operating system where AI can read and improve the company.
- Domain knowledge extraction is the compounding move because agents cannot improve a company whose context is locked in people's heads.
- Recursion appears when support transcripts, product usage, and internal feedback feed improvements back into docs, prompts, and product workflows.
- Burning tokens may become cheaper than adding headcount for repetitive coordination work, but only if context and verification are strong.
- Middle-management reporting work is vulnerable when the company state becomes queryable and agents can produce status, synthesis, and follow-up automatically.

## Open Questions
- What governance prevents [[concepts/Self-Improvement-Loop]] from silently changing company process in the wrong direction?
- How should [[concepts/Context-Governance]] handle authority, stale decisions, and conflicting playbooks?
- Which parts of management remain human-owned once reporting and coordination become AI-readable?
