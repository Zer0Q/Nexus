# ISO/IEC 22989:2022 — AI Concepts and Terminology

## Summary
ISO/IEC 22989:2022 defines AI concepts, terminology, and the AI System Life Cycle Model. Establishes shared vocabulary for AI governance, breaking down trustworthiness into verifiable characteristics (robustness, explainability, transparency).

## Core Concepts
- [[tools/AI-Governance]] -- shared language and frameworks for accountable AI systems
- [[tools/AI-System-Life-Cycle]] -- stages from inception through retirement, with continuous monitoring
- [[concepts/Trustworthiness]] -- decomposed into robustness, explainability, transparency
- [[tools/AI-Risk-Management]] -- ISO 23894 operationalizes the vocabulary defined in ISO 22989

## Key Insights
- "Babel Syndrome": lack of shared AI vocabulary is a compliance and security risk
- AI systems are defined by human-defined objectives — without them, the system isn't governed
- AI lifecycle differs fundamentally from traditional SDLC: dynamic performance, continuous V&V, ethical alignment
- ISO 22989 is the prerequisite for ISO 42001 — you cannot manage what you cannot define

## Open Questions
- How does the AI lifecycle model map to agentic development workflows?
- Can trustworthiness characteristics be automated as quality gates?
