# ISO 27001:2022 Annex A Controls

## Summary
ISO 27001:2022 restructured Annex A from 114 controls (14 categories) to 93 controls (4 categories), adding 11 new controls, merging 24 from the 2013 version, and revising 58 to align with current cybersecurity. The Statement of Applicability (SoA) is mandatory for certification and must list all implemented controls, justify inclusions, confirm implementation, and justify omissions. Controls are grouped into Organisational (37), People (8), Physical (14), and Technological (34).

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/ISO-27001-Annex-A]] -- 93 security controls demonstrating compliance with ISO 27001 6.1.3 (risk treatment) and Statement of Applicability
- [[concepts/Statement-of-Applicability]] -- mandatory document listing Annex A controls an org will implement, with justification for inclusions and omissions
- [[concepts/ISO-27001-Organisational-Controls]] -- 37 controls (A 5.1-5.37): policies, rules, processes, organisational structures for data protection
- [[concepts/ISO-27001-People-Controls]] -- 8 controls (A 6.1-6.8): HR management, personnel security, awareness and training
- [[concepts/ISO-27001-Physical-Controls]] -- 14 controls (A 7.1-7.13): entry systems, guest access, asset disposal, clear desk policies
- [[concepts/ISO-27001-Technological-Controls]] -- 34 controls (A 8.1-8.34): authentication, BUDR strategies, information logging, cybernetic regulations
- [[concepts/Control-Attribute-Taxonomy]] -- each control has suggested attributes for alignment with NIST, CIS controls, and other standards

## Key Insights
- 2022 change: 114 controls → 93 controls (24 merged, 58 revised, 11 new) — consolidation, not reduction in coverage
- The 4-category model (Organisational/People/Physical/Technological) replaces the 14-category model from 2013
- SoA is not optional — it's a mandatory step for ISO 27001 certification with 4 required elements
- Control attributes allow quick alignment with NIST and CIS controls using common industry language
- The core ISMS management processes remain unchanged; only the Annex A control set was updated
