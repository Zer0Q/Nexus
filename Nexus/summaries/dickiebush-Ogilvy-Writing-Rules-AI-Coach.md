# Ogilvy's 10 Writing Rules as AI Skill

## Summary
The article presents David Ogilvy's 10 writing rules from a 1982 memo to his staff at Ogilvy & Mather, then demonstrates how they were encoded into a Claude Skill that audits writing against all 10 principles. The skill produces severity-ranked violations with exact quotes and specific fixes, plus highlights what's working. The underlying thesis: writing principles are well-documented but scattered across decades of practice, making them hard to apply systematically — encoding them as an AI skill solves that gap.

## Core Concepts
- Ogilvy-Writing-Rules -- 10 writing principles from David Ogilvy's 1982 memo: read Roman-Raphaelson 3x, write how you talk, use short words/sentences/paragraphs, never use jargon, never write more than 2 pages, check quotations, wait a day before sending, get a colleague to improve it, clarify the desired action, don't write when you want action
- AI-Writing-Coach -- Claude Skill that audits writing against Ogilvy's 10 rules, producing severity-ranked violations (Critical/Moderate/Minor) with exact quotes and specific fixes
- Roman-Raphaelson -- classic writing book recommended by Ogilvy as required reading for all staff

## Key Insights
- Ogilvy generated over $864 million for his clients; his memo opens with "The better you write, the higher you will go in Ogilvy & Mather"
- Rule 2 ("Write the way you talk"): record yourself talking about a topic, transcribe, and start there — your voice already exists
- Rule 3 ("Use short words, sentences, paragraphs"): read everything aloud before publishing; getting caught up is a sign to simplify
- Rule 4 ("Never use jargon"): jargon hides lack of understanding; pretend you're writing to an 8th grader
- Rule 5 ("Never write more than 2 pages"): 99% of books should be blog posts, 99% of blog posts should be LinkedIn posts
- Rule 7 ("Never send on the day you write"): the single most important writing advice — give it room to breathe, read aloud the next morning
- The Claude Skill output format: one-line overall read, every violation sorted by severity with exact quote + why it's a problem + specific fix, plus what's working so you don't accidentally edit away good parts
