# AI Doesn't Make Anyone Good. It Makes Good People Harder to Catch

## Summary

A framework for understanding how AI is reshaping GTM (Go-To-Market) roles. Uses a two-axis model: technical skills (ability to work with technology) vs commercial skills (ability to navigate business dynamics). AI widens the gap between people already good at combining both. All client-facing technical roles are converging toward the Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) archetype. Two categories of AI tools drive the shift: reasoning/building tools (Claude, Cursor, Copilot) and orchestration platforms (Kizen, etc.). The key variable AI amplifies is technical curiosity.

## Core Concepts

- Forward-Deployed-Engineer — High technical depth + commercial instinct, owns full cycle end-to-end
- [[concepts/AI-Power-User-Gap]] — AI widens the gap between already-capable people and everyone else
- Technical-Commercial-Duality — Two-axis framework: technical skills vs commercial skills
- AI-Orchestration-Platforms — Tools that chain and deploy LLMs across real business workflows
- Technical-Curiosity-Moat — Willingness to tinker and build with actual client context

## Key Insights

- Solutions Engineering has the most to gain from AI (compresses 4-person engagement into 1)
- Sales Engineering ceiling is lower because work is more repeatable
- Implementation gets massive technical lift but smaller commercial gain
- The minimum viable technical bar for AEs is rising as products get more technical
- R&D roles are converging toward "builder" — one person who codes, designs, and ships

## Open Questions

- How do organizations measure the technical-commercial duality in hiring?
- What training programs effectively develop technical curiosity in non-engineers?
- How does the FDE model scale across different industries?
