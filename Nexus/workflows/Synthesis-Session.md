# Synthesis-Session

## Definition
A bi-weekly integrated understanding session per subject that reads across all concept notes to identify emergent patterns, the deepest connection in the subject, run predictive tests, perform gap analysis, and produce a synthesis document proving understanding rather than familiarity.

## Why It Matters
Active recall and spaced repetition build and maintain individual nodes. The synthesis session builds the integrated understanding that makes knowledge usable rather than just retained. A student who can explain how ten concepts interact has understood the subject.

## Key Steps
1. Pattern identification: read across all concept notes, find patterns not stated in any individual note
2. Deepest connection: identify the single most important relationship tying the most concepts together
3. Predictive test: generate three novel scenarios, ask the learner to predict outcomes, then evaluate predictions
4. Gap analysis: identify the three most important unstudied concepts that would most increase understanding
5. Synthesis document: write a 400-600 word integrated understanding document saved to the synthesis folder

## Tradeoffs
- Requires sufficient concept coverage before producing meaningful synthesis
- The synthesis document may reveal gaps that require additional study rounds
