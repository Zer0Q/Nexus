# Three-Zone-Vault

## Definition
A vault architecture with three zones representing stages of note processing: Zone 1 (Capture) for raw unprocessed material, Zone 2 (Active) for processed notes immediately useful for current work, and Zone 3 (Deep Archive) for completed projects and historical records.

## Why It Matters
Provides a lifecycle model for notes that enforces movement from raw capture toward active use or archival. Unlike static folder structures, zones represent processing stages -- notes must earn their place in Zone 2 by being rewritten, connected, and tied to active work.

## Key Ideas
- **Zone 1 - Capture**: Raw material, inbox of thinking, candidates for usefulness not yet useful
- **Zone 2 - Active**: Permanent notes in your own words, project notes, active decisions, reference notes
- **Zone 3 - Deep Archive**: Completed projects, resolved decisions, indexed for retrieval but not actively maintained
- Notes move through zones as they become more useful or lose relevance
- Zone 2 is the working layer; notes here must connect to at least one active project or decision

## Tradeoffs
- Simpler than the [[concepts/Four-Layer-Vault-Architecture]] but may lack granularity for complex vaults
- Requires discipline to actually move notes between zones
- Archive zone prevents vault bloat but risks losing context for future retrieval
