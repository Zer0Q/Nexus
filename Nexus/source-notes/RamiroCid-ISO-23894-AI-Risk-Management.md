# ISO/IEC 23894:2023 — AI Risk Management

## Summary
ISO/IEC 23894:2023 extends ISO 31000 risk management to the AI lifecycle, addressing emergent risks (algorithmic bias, model drift, adversarial vulnerabilities, explainability gaps). Connects with ISO 42001 (AIMS) and ISO 38507 (board governance).

## Core Concepts
- [[frameworks/AI-Risk-Management]] -- ISO 23894 extends traditional ERM to non-deterministic AI systems
- [[glossary/Model-Drift]] -- performance degradation as real-world data shifts over time
- [[concepts/Risk-by-Design]] -- proactive risk methodology integrated across the AI lifecycle
- [[glossary/Adversarial-Vulnerabilities]] -- data poisoning and model evasion bypass traditional defenses

## Key Insights
- AI introduces emergent risk domains that don't map to conventional cybersecurity or privacy definitions
- Risk management must span the full AI lifecycle: inception, data development, continuous monitoring
- ISO 23894 is the practical bridge between executive accountability (ISO 38507) and engineering reality
- Trust, not compliance, is the strategic ceiling — systematic risk management enables faster innovation
