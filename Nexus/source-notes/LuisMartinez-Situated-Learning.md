# Situated Learning Theory

## Summary
Situated learning theory (Lave & Wenger) proposes that knowledge is constructed through social practice within a community, not through abstract propositional acquisition. Key concepts: communities of practice, legitimate peripheral participation, and knowledge as participation rather than possession.

## Core Concepts
- [[concepts/Situated-Learning]] -- knowledge acquired through social practice in authentic contexts
- [[concepts/Communities-of-Practice]] -- groups where knowledge is co-constructed through shared participation
- [[glossary/Legitimate-Peripheral-Participation]] -- newcomers learn by gradually moving from periphery to core

## Key Insights
- Learning is inseparable from the social and cultural context in which it occurs
- Knowledge is participation, not possession — it's constructed through interaction, not transferred
- Four components: knowledge content, situational context, community of participants, participation process
- Modern corporate training uses communities of practice instead of static manuals
- Continuous learning through real problems generates better knowledge transfer than theoretical content

## Open Questions
