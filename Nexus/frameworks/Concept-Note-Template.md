# Concept-Note-Template

## Definition
A structured format for learning notes that captures one idea precisely enough to be tested on and connected to other ideas. Includes frontmatter with concept metadata, sections for definition in own words, mechanism, failure conditions, connections, and an active recall test question.

## Why It Matters
The template enforces the first act of retrieval practice: writing the concept in your own words. If you cannot write the "What It Is" section without looking at the source, you have already identified a gap. The template makes every note testable and connectable.

## Key Ideas
- Frontmatter: concept name, subject, source, date-added, review-due, connections array, mastery score
- What It Is: concept in your own words (not the source's words)
- Why It Matters: what problem this concept solves
- The Mechanism: how it works, what are the moving parts
- Where It Breaks Down: conditions where the concept fails or produces incorrect predictions
- Connections: links to other concept notes, added by Claude
- The Test: active recall question generated by Claude

## Tradeoffs
- More structure than a standard note; may feel heavy for quick captures
- The "Where It Breaks Down" section requires deeper understanding than most learners have initially
