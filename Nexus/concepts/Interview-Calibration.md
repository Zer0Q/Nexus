# Interview Calibration

## Definition
Proceso de alinear interviewers para evaluar consistentemente candidatos: scorecards, DRI (Directly Responsible Individual), reverse shadowing, y monitoring de interviewer variance.

## Why It Matters
Sin calibration, diferentes interviewers producen scores incomparables, llevando a bad hiring decisions. A escala de 100+ personas, la consistency del interview process es crítica para fairness y quality.

## Key Ideas
- Assign DRI to calibrate interviewers y review scorecards cuando múltiples interviewers
- Watch for interviewers who always grade YES but never STRONG YES/NO — probablemente uncalibrated
- A candidate con varios STRONG YESes y un NO es a menudo más interesante que YESes across board
- Constantly train new interviewers through reverse shadowing
- Monitor interviewing metrics: pass rates, interviewer variance, stage conversion, close rates
- Helps debug calibration problems antes de que causen bad hires
- Consistency y fairness vienen de stick a standard loop

## Tradeoffs
- Calibration overhead vs speed de hiring
- Standardized scores vs nuance de evaluaciones individuales
- Reverse shadowing consume time de senior engineers
- Scorecards pueden incentivar gaming del proceso
