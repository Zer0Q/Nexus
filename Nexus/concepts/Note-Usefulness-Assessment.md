# Note-Usefulness-Assessment

## Definition
An evaluation process that determines whether a captured note can contribute to any active project, decision, or writing topic. Notes that pass the assessment are processed into permanent form; those that fail are archived directly.

## Why It Matters
Without assessment, every capture enters the vault with equal weight. Assessment enforces the principle that notes must earn their place by connecting to active work. It is the gatekeeping mechanism that prevents vault bloat while ensuring useful notes are not lost.

## Key Ideas
- Assessment asks: can this note contribute to any active project, decision, or writing topic?
- If yes: mark for processing (rewrite in own words, connect to existing notes)
- If no: archive directly without processing
- A note is ready for Zone 2 when: written in own words, connected to at least one other Zone 2 note, contributes to at least one active project or decision, has a clear idea-based title
- Should be archived when: project/decision complete, information outdated, or never accessed in 6 months

## Tradeoffs
- Requires maintaining an up-to-date list of active projects and decisions (via CLAUDE.md or similar)
- Risk of archiving notes that would become useful for future, not-yet-defined work
