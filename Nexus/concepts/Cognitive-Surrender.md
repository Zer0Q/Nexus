# Cognitive Surrender

## Definition
Accepting loop output without judgment — using automated loops to avoid thinking rather than to move faster on work you understand deeply. The loop doesn't know the difference between these two uses, but the outcome is radically different.

## Why It Matters
When the loop runs itself, it's very tempting to stop having an opinion and just take whatever it gives back. Designing the loop is the cure when you do it with judgment, and the accelerant when you do it to avoid thinking — same action, opposite result. This is the human risk behind loop engineering, not a technical one.

## Key Ideas
- "Build the loop. But build it like someone who intends to stay the engineer, not just the person who presses go." — Addy Osmani
- Two people can build the exact same loop and get completely opposite results
- The loop changes the work, it does not delete you from it
- Your understanding still rots if you allow it — that's [[concepts/Comprehension-Debt]]
- Verification is still on you: a loop running unattended is also a loop making mistakes unattended
