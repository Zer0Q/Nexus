# Identity-Protective Cognition

## Definition
Psychological mechanism where people defend their self-concept to maintain consistency. When identity is threatened, the same fight-or-flight response activates as physical threats. Explains why people resist change even when it benefits them.

## Why It Matters
Identity protection is the primary barrier to behavior change. People don't reject information that contradicts their identity -- they literally feel threatened. Understanding this explains resistance in yourself and others.

## Key Ideas
- Identity cycle: goal -> perception -> learning -> action -> conditioning -> identity -> defense -> new goals
- Identity formed in childhood through reward/punishment from parents/culture
- Parents pass down culturally accepted ideas (Industrial age values)
- Physical survival needs met -> ideological survival begins (protecting beliefs)
- Threatened identity triggers same physiological response as physical threat
- Echo chambers and double-downing are identity defense, not rational disagreement
- Breaking the cycle between conditioning (step 6) and defense (step 7) requires conscious intervention

## Tradeoffs
- Identity provides psychological safety -- removing it creates anxiety
- Change requires temporary discomfort with uncertainty
