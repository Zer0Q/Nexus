# Quarterly Vault Review

## Definition
A periodic maintenance habit (every 3 months) that audits folder structure, tag taxonomy, archive status, and naming consistency. Keeps the organizational system accurate as the vault grows and evolves.

## Why It Matters
Organization degrades over time without maintenance. Tags accumulate that no longer reflect how you think. Folders that made sense in January don't reflect October's projects. The quarterly review pays back every time you find a note instantly.

## Key Ideas
- Folder audit: merge folders with <5 notes, remove unused categories
- Tag audit: remove tags on 1-2 notes, create MOCs for topics with 20+ notes
- Archive sweep: move completed projects, outdated references to ARCHIVE
- Naming consistency: batch rename to fix inconsistencies
- Takes 30 min to 2 hours depending on vault size
- Progressive reorganization beats starting over

## Tradeoffs
- Requires blocking time for maintenance
- Risk of over-organizing during review
- May reveal that the system needs fundamental changes, not just cleanup
