# Gap-Analysis-Study

## Definition
A diagnostic process that identifies the three most important concepts a learner has not yet studied but would most increase their understanding of what they already know. Based on what the learner knows and does not know.

## Why It Matters
Prevents inefficient studying by targeting the concepts that would produce the highest marginal increase in understanding. Rather than studying sequentially through a textbook, gap analysis directs study effort to the concepts that fill the most critical holes in the knowledge graph.

## Key Ideas
- Analyzes what is known and what is missing from the knowledge graph
- Identifies concepts whose absence most limits understanding of existing concepts
- Prioritizes by impact: which missing concept would most increase understanding of what is already there
- Part of the [[Synthesis-Session]] workflow, run every two weeks per subject
- Produces a targeted study list rather than a sequential syllabus

## Tradeoffs
- May skip foundational concepts in favor of high-impact connections
- Depends on Claude's domain knowledge to identify truly important gaps
