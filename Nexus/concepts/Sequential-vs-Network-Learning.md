# Sequential-vs-Network-Learning

## Definition
A comparison between linear study (reading in sequence, writing in sequence) and graph-based study (storing concepts as connected nodes in a network). Sequential study collects isolated facts; network study builds understanding through connections.

## Why It Matters
Traditional note-taking is linear: concept A on page 12, concept B on page 20, concept C on page 45. Nothing in writing down concept C connects it to concept A even when deeply related. The knowledge graph approach changes the structure from a collection of nodes to a network.

## Key Ideas
- Sequential study: read in order, write in order, connections not created unless deliberate
- Network study: every new concept connected to existing concepts, stored non-sequentially
- The connections exist in the subject matter; they do not exist in your notes unless deliberately created
- Claude accelerates network learning by finding connections that sequential reading cannot produce
- The second student (who built connections) is not smarter -- they built the connections

## Tradeoffs
- Network learning requires more upfront effort per concept
- Sequential reading may be faster for initial exposure to a new subject
