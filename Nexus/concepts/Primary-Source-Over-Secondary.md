# Primary Source Over Secondary

## Definition
The research principle of going directly to original data -- the paper, the on-chain data, the institutional filing, the raw clinical record -- rather than relying on information that has been filtered through commentary, summaries, or social media.

## Why It Matters
Information that has passed through multiple layers of interpretation accumulates distortion at each step. By the time an insight reaches your feed, it is either already consensus (too late) or wrong. Primary source research preserves signal and filters out noise with confidence.

## Key Ideas
- "Information that has already been filtered through too many layers is not information. It is noise with confidence."
- Applied to medical research: use the full clinical history, not a summary
- Applied to crypto research: read on-chain data, not CT commentary
- Applied to general research: read the paper, not the blog post about the paper
- If something is already consensus on social media, you are already late to it

## Tradeoffs
- Primary source analysis takes more time and expertise
- Not all primary sources are equally reliable
- Requires the ability to evaluate raw data without guidance
