# Capture-Use-Gap

## Definition
The gap between capturing information and actively using it. Capturing has no stakes -- it feels productive but requires no synthesis. Using requires active retrieval, synthesis with current knowledge, and transformation into output.

## Why It Matters
Most note-taking systems optimize for capture (files, folders, tags, search) while ignoring use entirely. The Capture-Use-Gap explains why vaults accumulate without compounding: notes sit in folders, found by accident months later, and never become anything.

## Key Ideas
- Capturing is easy because it has no stakes; using requires active retrieval at the moment of need
- The gap is the distance between passive collection and active synthesis
- Systems designed for collection: files, folders, tags, search
- Systems designed for synthesis: workflows that surface the right information at the right moment
- Closing the gap requires structural decisions that privilege use over capture

## Tradeoffs
- Systems optimized for use require more upfront design than systems optimized for capture
- The discomfort of not having a quick capture path may cause some useful information to be lost
