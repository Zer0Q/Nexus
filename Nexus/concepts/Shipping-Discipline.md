# Shipping Discipline

## Definition
The standing order with yourself to produce and publish output from a knowledge vault on a regular cadence -- every week, no matter how rough the draft. It is the single differentiator between a hobby vault (4000 notes, $0 revenue) and a revenue-producing vault ($35K/month).

## Why It Matters
Most people who build a vault never reach revenue because they never ship from it. They capture, run briefings, tweak CLAUDE.md, and post screenshots of their graph view. They never open the output folder. Structure, capture, and tools are replicable. Shipping discipline is not.

## Key Ideas
- The vault does not produce revenue by existing -- it produces revenue when you run the output workflow and ship the result
- Shipping cadence: once a day, twice a week, or once a Sunday -- consistency matters more than frequency
- The vault rewards shipping discipline disproportionately
- Six months of capturing + six months of shipping = a context layer AND a body of public work nobody else can copy
- That combination is the $35K/month moat
- No better prompts, no better model, no fancier folder structure can substitute for shipping discipline

## Tradeoffs
- Shipping rough drafts risks reputational cost if audience expects polished work
- Early output may be poor quality until the vault has enough depth to feed the generator
- The psychological barrier of publishing is the hardest part to overcome
