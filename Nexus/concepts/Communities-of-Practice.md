# Communities of Practice

Groups where knowledge is co-constructed through shared participation and practice. Newcomers learn through [[Legitimate-Peripheral-Participation]], gradually moving from periphery to core.

## Key Characteristics
- Knowledge emerges from shared practice, not top-down instruction
- Members learn from each other through interaction and collaboration
- Gradual integration: newcomers start at the periphery and move inward

## Application in Knowledge Management
- Corporate training: replace static manuals with practice communities
- Open source: contributors learn by participating in real projects
- AI communities: practitioners share prompts, techniques, and workflows
