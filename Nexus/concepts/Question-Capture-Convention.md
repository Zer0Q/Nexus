# Question-Capture-Convention

## Definition
A capture convention that explicitly records questions raised by information. Format: [THE INFORMATION] THIS RAISES THE QUESTION: [the question this generates].

## Why It Matters
Questions are often more useful than answers in a knowledge system. A question surface workflow can search for notes that might answer captured questions, turning passive information into active inquiry.

## Key Ideas
- Questions persist as search targets across the vault
- A question note can be answered by multiple future captures
- The question surface workflow searches for notes that might answer captured questions
- Capturing questions prevents them from being lost in the flow of information

## Tradeoffs
- May lead to question hoarding if questions are never actively pursued
- Requires a mechanism to track which questions have been answered
