# Knowledge Mapping

## Definition
The practice of creating visual and structural representations of how ideas, concepts, and information relate to each other within a knowledge base. Goes beyond simple tagging to capture the semantic relationships between pieces of knowledge.

## Why It Matters
Enables discovery of non-obvious connections, helps identify gaps in understanding, and provides navigation paths through complex knowledge domains.

## Key Ideas
- Graph-based visualization shows concept density and connectivity
- Maps reveal clusters of related ideas that inform index creation
- Regular mapping exercises surface orphaned notes and stale connections
