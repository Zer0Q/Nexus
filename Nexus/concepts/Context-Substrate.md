# Context Substrate

## Definition
The machine-usable substance of enterprise context, composed of three tightly integrated parts: (1) AI-ready data and knowledge graph -- trusted representation of data and knowledge assets; (2) Semantics and ontology -- shared definitions and relationship structures; (3) Skills -- reusable procedures and norms.

## Why It Matters
Each part answers a question an agent needs before acting: "What data can I trust?" (data + KG), "What do things mean and how are they connected?" (semantics), "How does work get done here and what's allowed?" (skills). You cannot have one without the others.

## Key Ideas
- Data without semantics is uninterpretable
- Semantics without data describes a business nobody can query
- Both without skills describe how the company works without being able to operate it
- Canonical knowledge (strategy docs, brand voice, org charts) is an underrated substrate component
- Ontology enables cross-system reasoning; without it, retrieval is brittle
- Skills are the procedural knowledge primitive: reusable, versionable, testable

## Tradeoffs
- Canonical vs operational knowledge -- which takes priority when they conflict?
- Ontology curation speed vs accuracy
- Skill granularity -- fine-grained skills are flexible but numerous; coarse skills are fewer but rigid
