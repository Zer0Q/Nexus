## Definition

Multi-perspective research is a method where you simulate multiple expert perspectives on a topic, map their contradictions, synthesize findings, and self-critique. The key insight: different perspectives see different things, catching blind spots that a single prompt never reveals.

## The 4-Step Workflow

1. **Multi-Perspective Scan:** Simulate 5 expert perspectives (practitioner, academic, skeptic, economist, historian). Each gives core position, strongest evidence, and one unique insight.
2. **Contradiction Map:** Map where perspectives fight. Find direct contradictions, strongest/weakest evidence, the question that would resolve the biggest contradiction, what everyone agrees on, and what nobody addressed.
3. **Synthesis:** Pull everything into a briefing: CEO summary, key findings ranked by reliability, hidden connection, actionable insight, frontier question.
4. **Peer Review:** Grade your own work: confidence scores, weakest link, bias check, missing perspective, overall grade.

## Origin

Stanford's STORM (Synthesis of Topic Outlines through Retrieval and Multi-perspective Question Asking), published at NAACL 2024. Articles built from multiple perspectives were 25% more organized and 10% broader in coverage.

## Key Insight

"If all 5 perspectives agree, it is probably true. If nobody addressed a topic, you just found the gap in the entire field."
