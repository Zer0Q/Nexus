# Gemba

## Definition
"Go to the real place" — the Lean practice of observing work where it actually happens, with the people who actually do it, rather than diagnosing problems in meetings with managers and stakeholders.

## Why It Matters
Most transformation projects diagnose process and data issues in meetings with people who aren't the ones doing the work. Managers know what the process should be; operators know how it actually works. The gap between those two is where problems live.

## Key Ideas
- **Wrong room, wrong people:** most transformations diagnose issues with managers, process owners, and strategic stakeholders — but they're not the ones entering data or handling exceptions
- **Frontline knowledge:** the person entering data knows things no dashboard will tell you; the person handling exceptions knows exactly where the process breaks
- **Official vs. actual process:** which fields confuse everyone, which steps get skipped, which parts of the official process exist only in theory
- **Bring them in from day one:** AI transformation is change management, and people belong at the center of it
- **Not a one-time visit:** ongoing engagement with frontline workers who flag confusing categories, suggest better designs, and catch problems management never would have seen
- **Weekly data review meetings:** technicians reviewing their own data gave them ownership and turned them from endpoints into system improvers

## Tradeoffs
- Takes time away from "efficient" meeting-based planning
- Frontline feedback can challenge assumptions held by decision-makers
