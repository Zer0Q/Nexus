# Active-Recall-Learning

## Definition
A study method where the learner deliberately retrieves information from memory rather than passively reviewing it. The discomfort of not being able to remember is evidence that learning is happening, not evidence of failure.

## Why It Matters
Research by Roediger and Karpicke showed students who tested themselves through active recall retained significantly more information a week later than those who spent the same time in passive review. Combining with spaced repetition improved exam performance by 15-20%.

## Key Ideas
- Writing a concept in your own words is the first act of retrieval practice
- Active recall questions should require application, not definition
- A question that cannot be answered by memorizing the definition tests understanding
- Self-generated questions are often too easy; Claude generates harder, application-based questions
- Answer each question before reading the evaluation; cover the evaluation section

## Tradeoffs
- Feels harder and less comfortable than passive review
- Requires generating or accessing quality questions
- May reveal gaps that feel discouraging
