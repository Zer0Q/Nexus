## Definition

Problem shaping is the skill of taking an ambiguous customer pain point and translating it into a clear, bounded problem statement that agents or teams can act on without constant clarification. It is THE skill for PMs in the age of agents.

## Why It Matters

When agents can produce working code from a well-formed problem, the PM's job shifts from translating for engineers to forming intent clearly enough that agents can act on it directly. The spec and the prototype are becoming the same thing.

## Components

- **Ambiguity tolerance:** Hold ambiguity longer while exploring the solution space
- **Constraint articulation:** Identify which constraints actually shape the solution
- **Success definition:** Articulate what success looks like concretely
- **Context curation:** Feed agents the right context (user, problem in their words, examples, failures, constraints)
