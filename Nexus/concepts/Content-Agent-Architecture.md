# Content Agent Architecture

## Definition
The architecture for AI content agents that goes beyond voice replication to include taste, thesis, proof, and forbidden-pattern files. Voice makes it sound like you; these four elements make it think like you.

## Why It Matters
Voice replication alone produces content that sounds right but lacks the author's actual perspective, standards, and intellectual boundaries. True content agents need the full cognitive profile, not just the surface style.

## Key Ideas
- Voice: how you sound -- tone, vocabulary, sentence structure
- Taste: what you consider good vs bad -- aesthetic and quality judgment
- Thesis: what you believe -- core arguments and positions
- Proof: what evidence you accept -- standards for claims and citations
- Forbidden patterns: what you never do -- phrases, arguments, or styles to avoid
- Content agents need all four to produce authentic, high-quality output
- Building in public creates community feedback that refines the agent over time

## Tradeoffs
- Authenticity vs consistency -- too rigid a profile limits creative range
- Profile specificity vs generalization -- narrow profiles are authentic but inflexible
- Manual curation vs self-evolving profiles
