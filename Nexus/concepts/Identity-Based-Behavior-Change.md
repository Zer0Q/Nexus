# Identity-Based Behavior Change

## Definition
Approach to lasting change that targets identity transformation rather than surface-level goal setting. The premise: if you want a specific outcome, you must adopt the lifestyle that creates it before you reach it.

## Why It Matters
Most people fail at goals because they try to build new behaviors on an unchanged identity foundation. Discipline fades when the underlying self-concept hasn't shifted.

## Key Ideas
- Changing actions is second-order; changing who you are is first-order
- Successful people don't "grind" -- their lifestyle feels natural because identity aligned first
- If you don't adopt the lifestyle that led to the goal, you revert when motivation fades
- When identity truly changes, misaligned habits become "disgusting" -- you self-correct
- Current standards are acceptable because you're not fully aware of what they compound into

## Tradeoffs
- Identity change is slower and more uncomfortable than goal-setting
- Requires confronting why you haven't changed already (unconscious goals)
