# AI Literacy

## Definition
AI literacy is the practical ability to understand what AI systems can and cannot do, how they affect a role or workflow, what risks they introduce, and how to use or oversee them responsibly.

## Why It Matters
AI literacy is becoming both a governance requirement and an operational capability. Without it, people over-trust outputs, miss system limitations, mishandle data, or fail to escalate risk.

## Key Ideas
- Literacy should be role-specific and risk-based, not a generic training module.
- Operational users need different depth than developers, risk owners, or executives.
- Training records and refresh cycles matter for compliance.
- Literacy connects governance obligations to day-to-day behavior.
