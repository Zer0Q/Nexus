# Research-First Architecture

## Definition
Building a research agent as the input intelligence layer that feeds every other agent. Everything it gathers from the world becomes training data to refine builds over time. Research should route findings into workflows: coding, content, marketing, competitive intel, consulting signals, and product ideas.

## Why It Matters
Raw links, feeds, and summaries are not enough. Agents need structured, verified, source-backed information they can act on. The research agent is the foundation that makes all downstream agents more capable.

## Key Ideas
- Research agent as the primary input layer -- everything flows through it
- Structured output: verified, source-backed information, not raw links
- Routing: findings distributed to relevant downstream agents based on content
- Research should not die in a doc -- it must trigger action in workflows
- Continuous improvement: research data refines agent builds over time
- Scraping ≠ research: raw data collection is not the same as structured intelligence

## Tradeoffs
- Research depth vs breadth -- deep analysis of few topics vs shallow scan of many
- Real-time vs batch research -- frequency of intelligence updates
- Source diversity vs echo chamber -- avoiding self-reinforcing loops
