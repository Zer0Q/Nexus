# Visual Management

## Definition
Making process and data issues visible and impossible to ignore, so that gaps between actual behavior and expected standards become self-evident to everyone involved. A Lean technique that closes the loop between what people do and what everyone can see.

## Why It Matters
Most broken processes survive precisely because nobody can see them breaking. Visual management creates immediate social pressure for compliance without requiring training, memos, or enforcement. In the author's experience, bad data disappeared almost overnight after a real-time monitor was placed in the operations office.

## Key Ideas
- **Real-time visibility:** large monitor showing exactly what was happening on the shop floor — what was broken, what was being worked on, what was sitting untouched
- **Self-correction:** people didn't suddenly care about data quality; the gap between what they did and what everyone could see closed
- **Not about blame:** the point is to expose the process so it can be discussed, understood, and fixed
- **Behavior change through visibility:** when people see the immediate impact of their actions, behavior changes on its own
- **AI as visual management at scale:** an AI agent can surface missing information, report repeating exceptions, and trigger alerts for process deviations
- **Before automation:** visual management should precede AI automation — make problems visible before trying to automate the solution

## Tradeoffs
- Can create social pressure that feels like surveillance if not implemented with psychological safety
- Requires a culture where visibility leads to improvement, not punishment
