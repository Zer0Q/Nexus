# Throughput Accounting

## Definition
Management accounting system based on three metrics: Throughput (rate of generating money through sales), Inventory/Investment (money tied up in the system), and Operating Expense (money spent to convert Inventory to Throughput). Goal: maximize T, minimize I and OE.

## Why It Matters
Traditional cost accounting incentivizes perverse behaviors (overproduction to reduce unit cost). Throughput Accounting aligns metrics with actual system performance — growth of revenue is theoretically unlimited, while cost cutting has a physical floor of zero.

## Key Ideas
- Throughput (T): Revenue minus totally variable costs — maximize
- Inventory/Investment (I): Money tied up in WIP, equipment, infrastructure — minimize
- Operating Expense (OE): Money spent to convert I into T — minimize
- Focus on increasing T, not reducing OE — revenue growth has no ceiling, cost cuts do

## Tradeoffs
- Requires mental shift from traditional accounting (department-level cost centers)
- Harder to communicate to stakeholders used to P&L statements
