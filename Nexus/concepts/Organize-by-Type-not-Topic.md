# Organize by Type, Not Topic

## Definition
Organizing notes by the kind of thinking they represent (observations, reactions, patterns, questions, numbers) rather than by their subject matter. Enables cross-domain connections that topic-based organization would prevent.

## Why It Matters
When organized by topic, a note about attention economics in crypto and a note about attention mechanics in gaming live in separate folders and never encounter each other. When organized by type, they both land in the patterns folder and AI can find the connection.

## Key Ideas
- Type categories: observations, reactions, patterns, questions, numbers, references
- Topic-based organization silos knowledge; type-based organization enables cross-pollination
- AI synthesis works better when similar thinking types are co-located
- Does not replace topic tags -- types and topics are orthogonal dimensions
- The single most important architectural decision in vault design

## Tradeoffs
- Less intuitive for humans used to topic-based filing
- Requires discipline to categorize by thinking type consistently
- May need topic tags as a secondary dimension for retrieval
