# ISA-18.2 Standard

## Definition
ANSI/ISA-18.2 (2016) is the authoritative international standard for management of alarm systems in process industries, covering the full alarm lifecycle from design through ongoing maintenance and modification. Nearly identical to IEC 62682.

## Why It Matters
Provides the framework that regulatory bodies (OSHA, EPA, FDA, PHMSA) recognize as generally accepted good engineering practice (RAGAGEP). Supported by eight technical reports that provide detailed "how-to" guidance for each lifecycle stage.

## Key Ideas
- **Published 2009, updated 2016** — designed not to conflict with EEMUA 191 or NAMUR NE102
- **Lifecycle stages:** philosophy, identification, rationalization, detailed design, implementation, operation, maintenance, monitoring/assessment, management of change, audit
- **Alarm philosophy required:** documented framework for design decisions, performance benchmarks, operator expectations
- **Performance monitoring:** essential requirements for system design, alarm prioritization, and HMI design guidelines
- **Technical reports (TRs):** eight TRs provide practical "how-to" guidance aligned to each lifecycle stage
- **Relationship to other standards:** IEC 62682 is nearly identical (slightly modified ISA version); EEMUA 191 is a guideline, not a standard; API RP1167 (pipelines) derived from ISA-18.2

## Tradeoffs
- Standard describes "what" not "how" — TRs needed for implementation details
- Requires organizational commitment beyond document compliance
