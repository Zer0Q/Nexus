# Predictive-Test-Method

## Definition
A testing approach where the learner is presented with novel scenarios they have not studied and asked to predict what would happen based on their knowledge graph. Claude then evaluates whether the prediction is correct and why.

## Why It Matters
Tests genuine understanding rather than memorization. If you can predict outcomes in novel situations using your accumulated knowledge, you have moved beyond familiarity to comprehension. This is the hardest test the system produces.

## Key Ideas
- Generate three novel scenarios not covered in existing concept notes
- Ask the learner to predict what would happen and why
- Do not reveal the answers; let the learner predict first
- Evaluate whether the prediction is correct and what it reveals about understanding
- A correct prediction demonstrates that the knowledge graph is structurally sound

## Tradeoffs
- Requires Claude to have domain knowledge beyond the vault to evaluate predictions
- May feel intimidating for learners early in the process
