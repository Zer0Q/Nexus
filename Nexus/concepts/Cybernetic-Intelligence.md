# Cybernetic Intelligence

## Definition
Intelligence defined as the ability to get what you want out of life through feedback loops: set goal, act, sense position, compare to goal, adjust. High intelligence = ability to iterate, persist, and understand the big picture.

## Why It Matters
Reframes intelligence from static IQ to dynamic capability. Anyone can improve their "intelligence" by building better feedback loops and learning from mistakes faster.

## Key Ideas
- Cybernetics: "the art of getting what you want" (Greek: kybernetikos = to steer)
- Feedback loop: goal -> act -> sense -> compare -> adjust -> repeat
- Success formula: agency x opportunity x intelligence
- High intelligence: iterate, persist, understand system from meta-perspective
- Low intelligence: inability to learn from mistakes, get stuck rather than solve
- Examples: ship correcting course, thermostat regulating, pancreas managing glucose
- Intelligence = trial and error with persistence and adaptation

## Tradeoffs
- Feedback loops require honest sensing (ego often distorts feedback)
- Opportunity constraint: high intelligence + low opportunity = limited results
