# Browsable Knowledge

## Definition
Knowledge organized in a navigable, human-readable format that supports serendipitous discovery through browsing, as opposed to query-only systems that require specific questions.

## Why It Matters
Humans discover connections through exploration, not just targeted queries. Browsable knowledge enables lateral thinking and unexpected insights that query-time systems alone cannot provide.

## Key Ideas
- Wiki pages, linked notes, and visual graphs enable browsing
- Write-time systems naturally produce browsable output
- Query-time systems lack this without a [[Compilation-Agent]]
- Serendipity is a feature, not a bug, in knowledge work
- The [[Graph-Knowledge-Layer]] bridges browsability with reliability

## Tradeoffs
- Browsable views can become stale without regular refresh
- Generating browsable layers adds complexity
