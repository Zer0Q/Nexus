# Spaced Repetition

## Definition
A learning technique that schedules review of material at increasing intervals based on the [[concepts/Ebbinghaus-Forgetting-Curve]]. Each successful recall extends the interval before the next review; each failure resets it. The goal is to review material at the point of near-forgetfulness.

## Key Properties
- Standard intervals: 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days, 30 days, then 60 days
- Mastery score drives interval adjustment
- More efficient than massed practice (cramming) or fixed-schedule review
- Combined with active recall, improves exam performance by 15-20%
