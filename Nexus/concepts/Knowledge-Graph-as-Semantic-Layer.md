# Knowledge Graph as Semantic Layer

## Definition
A structured network of conceptual entities and their relationships that enables AI agents to interpret the meaning of data in different business contexts. Encodes contextual modifiers that cause meanings to shift across domains.

## Why It Matters
Enables AI to reason with contextual understanding that structured data alone cannot provide. Companies with semantic layers built on knowledge graphs roll out new AI use cases in weeks, not months, because 50%+ of semantic structure can be reused.

## Key Ideas
- Identifies conceptual entities experts rely on when reasoning
- Articulates relationships connecting entities
- Encodes contextual modifiers (region, product line, customer segment)
- Enterprise knowledge graphs embed human expertise into platform connective tissue
- Human-in-the-loop review reinforces the graph with each new edge case
- Example: cosmetics company scaled regulatory evaluations from hundreds to 40K/month with 100% accuracy

## Tradeoffs
- Building requires domain expert time and AI engineering
- Graph maintenance as business context evolves
- Risk of encoding incorrect assumptions if not validated
