# Boundary Spanning

## Definition
Practices and actions that connect knowledge across organizational silos, internal and external communities, and different stakeholder groups. Boundary-spanning enables knowledge to flow between communities of practice, knowledge centres, and networks.

## Why It Matters
Organizations naturally develop silos — colleague, unit, or working group boundaries that restrict knowledge flow. Boundary-spanning practices cut across these divides, serving as motivational anchors for curiosity and engagement.

## Key Ideas
- Stakeholder and audience mapping: identify who holds relevant knowledge
- Network expansion: connect with external communities and professionals
- Professional exchanges: cross-organization knowledge sharing
- Currently limited in reach — identified as a key area for improvement
- Essential for community success in projects that span multiple domains

## Tradeoffs
- Requires resources (time, relationships, trust)
- Risk of diluting community focus by spanning too many boundaries
- External connections may introduce conflicting practices or standards
