# Information over Data

## Definition
Presenting contextual meaning alongside raw process values so operators can quickly assess whether conditions are normal or abnormal, rather than requiring them to interpret raw numbers against memory or documentation.

## Why It Matters
A pressure reading of "900 psi" is meaningless without context. By showing normal range indicators alongside process variables, operators can immediately assess whether values are trending toward abnormal conditions and take proactive action.

## Key Ideas
- **Raw data vs. information:** "900 psi" is data; "900 psi with normal range 850-950" is information
- **Normal range indicators:** visual bands or zones showing expected operating range alongside process variables
- **Proactive response:** operators can see trends away from normal and adjust before alarms trigger
- **Embedded trends:** always-visible historical trends on important variables enable proactive adjustments
- **Reduces cognitive load:** operator doesn't need to recall normal values or consult documentation
- **Part of High-Performance HMI:** core principle alongside grayscale design and display hierarchy

## Tradeoffs
- Requires more display design effort than simple value displays
- Normal ranges must be maintained and updated as processes change
