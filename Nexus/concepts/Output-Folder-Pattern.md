# Output-Folder-Pattern

## Definition
A vault folder dedicated to finished work -- articles, reports, decisions, frameworks -- produced from notes. Creates a visible connection between the notes captured and the work they produce.

## Why It Matters
Most note-taking systems never include an output layer. Without it, there is no visible record of which notes were generative and which were never used. Over time the Output folder shows the actual contribution rate of the vault, informing future capture decisions.

## Key Ideas
- Lives alongside note zones, not separate from the vault
- Contains finished pieces produced from notes, not drafts
- Shows which notes contributed to output and which did not
- Informs capture conventions: capture more of what has been generative, less of what is consistently unused
- After 90 days the Output folder becomes a calibration tool for the entire system

## Tradeoffs
- Adds maintenance burden: output must be saved to the vault, not just published externally
- May duplicate content already published elsewhere
