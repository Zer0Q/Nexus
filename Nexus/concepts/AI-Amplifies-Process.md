# AI Amplifies Process

## Definition
AI doesn't fix weak processes — it scales them faster and further than any system before. A broken manual process fails slowly and locally; a broken automated process fails fast and at scale. Automating on top of a shaky foundation industrializes the mess.

## Why It Matters
The stakes are higher with AI than with any previous technology. A supervisor forgetting one work order causes small, local damage. An AI system propagating the same error across thousands of transactions causes systemic failure. The cost of a broken process is multiplied by the speed and scale of automation.

## Key Ideas
- **Broken manual process:** fails slowly and locally — one supervisor forgets a work order, one machine's downtime is wrong
- **Broken automated process:** fails fast and at scale — same error multiplied across thousands of transactions
- **"Industrializing the mess":** automating on top of a shaky foundation preserves and amplifies existing problems
- **AI hasn't changed the rule, only raised the cost:** "Broken process in, broken AI out" is a sharper version of "garbage in, garbage out"
- **Process maturity matters:** AI should be the enabler, not the starting point — process must be stable, clear, and owned before automation
- **Data is a symptom, not the cause:** poor data is rarely the root cause; it's a symptom of something that happened much earlier in the process

## Tradeoffs
- Waiting for perfect processes delays AI benefits — balance between readiness and momentum
- Some processes can improve iteratively alongside AI deployment
