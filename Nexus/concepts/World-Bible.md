# World Bible

## Definition
A World Bible is the canonical reference document for a fictional universe, covering characters, locations, factions, timeline, rules, aesthetics, and unresolved creative decisions.

## Why It Matters
Large creative projects need a shared source of truth so writers, designers, developers, and artists can build in the same universe without contradictions. A good World Bible turns scattered lore into production-grade context.

## Key Ideas
- Captures both factual canon and the logic that makes the world coherent.
- Includes gaps and contradictions as explicit work items.
- Serves writers, game designers, artists, and interactive systems.
- Can be derived from a [[concepts/Knowledge-Graph]] and queried through [[concepts/Agentic-GraphRAG]].
