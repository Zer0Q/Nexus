# AI-Assisted Medical Research

## Definition
Using AI models as supplementary analytical tools for medical professionals to process clinical data, surface treatment options, and generate research-grade reports that doctors review, adopt, or discard. The AI does not make final treatment decisions.

## Why It Matters
Medical professionals cannot keep every clinical trial, paper, bibliography, and raw data point in their heads. AI can process this volume in minutes, clearing the ground for doctors to consider paths they may not have explored. In life-or-death cases, the risk-reward favors trying AI assistance.

## Key Ideas
- AI is a tool for the doctor, not a replacement
- The doctor reviews, adopts, or discards the AI report
- Produces actionable outputs: additional tests, possible exams, treatment paths
- Clarification: AI does not perform miracles; it processes data more exhaustively
- The value proposition: "If it helps the doctor, it helps the patient. If it doesn't, all we've lost is some time and tokens."

## Tradeoffs
- Liability: who is responsible if AI-suggested treatment fails?
- Models can hallucinate medical information
- Access cost: top models are expensive, creating equity issues
- Requires medical expertise to evaluate AI output
