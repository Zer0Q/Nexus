# Teleological Behavior

## Definition
All behavior is goal-oriented, even when the goals are unconscious. Self-sabotage, procrastination, and resistance all serve hidden purposes -- protection from judgment, maintaining safety, avoiding failure.

## Why It Matters
Understanding that behavior serves goals (even harmful ones) reframes "lack of discipline" as a rational response to unconscious objectives. Real change requires changing goals, not just willpower.

## Key Ideas
- Every action serves a goal, conscious or unconscious
- Procrastination may serve: protection from judgment, avoiding failure visibility
- Staying in dead-end job may serve: safety, predictability, social acceptance
- Goals are projections that act as perception lenses -- they determine what you notice
- "Trust only movement" (Adler) -- behavior reveals true goals more than stated intentions
- Surface-level goals often serve unconscious goals that harm you

## Tradeoffs
- Uncovering unconscious goals requires uncomfortable self-honesty
- Knowing your hidden goals doesn't automatically change them
