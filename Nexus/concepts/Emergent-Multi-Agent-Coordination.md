# Emergent Multi-Agent Coordination

## Definition
Coordination behavior that arises organically from simple economic incentives (wealth flow, rent, birth, bankruptcy) rather than explicit orchestration protocols, producing adaptive multi-agent populations without central control.

## Why It Matters
Most multi-agent stacks rely on hand-designed orchestration (explicit prompts, state machine graphs, role switches). Emergent coordination eliminates the need for engineers to manually design interaction topologies — the system discovers them through selection pressure.

## Key Ideas
- Coordination is an emergent property of selection, not an engineered protocol
- Interaction topology sharpens over time: the system gets better at which sequences of agents act
- Resource-aware behaviors emerge: societies learn when to act cautiously vs. aggressively based on workspace state
- Role specialization emerges without templates: agents discover useful specializations through wealth accumulation
- Similar patterns observed in pre-LLM multi-agent work (OpenAI Hide-and-Seek)

## Tradeoffs
- Emergent behaviors can be hard to predict or debug
- Early exploration phases can produce chaotic or suboptimal coordination
- Requires sufficient population diversity for meaningful emergence
