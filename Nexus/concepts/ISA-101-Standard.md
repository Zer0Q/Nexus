# ISA-101 Standard

## Definition
ANSI/ISA-101.01-2015 is the lifecycle standard for Human-Machine Interfaces in process automation systems, providing guidelines for HMI design, implementation, operations, and maintenance. ANSI-approved in July 2015 after 12 years of development (tasked in 2003).

## Why It Matters
Addresses the fact that most installed HMIs are not designed with operator situational awareness in mind. Provides a vendor-independent framework for effective HMI design that reduces engineering costs, improves operator effectiveness, and reduces plant incidents.

## Key Ideas
- **Lifecycle approach:** covers standards development, design, implementation, operations phases
- **Standards phase:** unified HMI philosophy, human factors guidelines, functional requirements, common style guides
- **Design phase:** hardware/software requirements, console design, display design documentation
- **Implementation phase:** building HMI in target platform, testing, documentation, training
- **Operations phase:** management of change, maintenance, decommissioning
- **Display types:** L1 (overall realm of control), L2 (unit operation), L3 (task detail), L4 (diagnostics)
- **Working groups:** WG1 (Philosophy & Style Guide), WG2 (Usability/Performance), WG3 (Mobile HMIs), WG4 (Machine HMIs)
- **Cost reduction:** standardization reduces $10,000+ per page engineering costs
- **Applies to:** continuous, batch, and discrete processes

## Tradeoffs
- Adoption requires cultural shift and retraining
- Legacy DCS migration can be expensive
- Most plants lack L2/L3 displays — significant redesign effort needed
