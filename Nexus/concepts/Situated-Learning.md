# Situated Learning

Knowledge is constructed through social practice within authentic contexts, not through abstract propositional acquisition. Proposed by Jean Lave and Etienne Wenger within the context of [[Communities-of-Practice]].

## Core Thesis
- Learning is inseparable from the social and cultural context in which it occurs
- Knowledge is participation, not possession
- The learner is an active participant in both acquiring and creating knowledge

## Four Components (Rankin)
1. **Knowledge Content** -- practical application, not theoretical memorization
2. **Situational Context** -- authentic environment providing necessary experience
3. **Community of Participants** -- knowledge co-constructed through interaction
4. **Participation Process** -- ideas exchanged, knowledge built collectively

## Modern Applications
- Corporate training: communities of practice replace static manuals
- AI-assisted learning: tools can create virtual practice environments
- Continuous learning through real problems generates better knowledge transfer
