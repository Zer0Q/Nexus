# Six-Month Revenue Curve

## Definition
The compounding revenue trajectory of a knowledge vault over six months of consistent capture and shipping. Revenue does not appear on Day 7 -- it builds as the vault accumulates context depth, which improves output quality, which increases audience trust and revenue.

## Why It Matters
The six-month curve is the moat. Anyone can install Obsidian today. Almost nobody will still be capturing in 90 days. The people who persist own a context layer no late mover can replicate. The curve rewards patience and consistency over initial speed.

## Key Ideas
- Months 1-2: Building the vault, low output, minimal revenue. Most people quit here.
- Months 3-4: Context depth improves, output quality rises, first revenue appears.
- Months 5-6: Compounding kicks in. The vault has enough depth to produce consistently high-quality output. Revenue accelerates.
- The 90-day mark is the critical filter -- those who survive it own a compounding advantage
- Six months of capturing + six months of shipping = context layer + public work nobody can copy
- The curve is non-linear: early months are investment, later months are payoff

## Tradeoffs
- Six months of investment before meaningful returns requires financial runway
- The curve assumes consistent capture AND shipping -- gaps in either flatten the trajectory
- Platform changes (algorithm shifts, policy changes) can disrupt the curve mid-flight
