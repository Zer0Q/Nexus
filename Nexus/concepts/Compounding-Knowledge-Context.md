# Compounding Knowledge Context

## Definition
The phenomenon where a knowledge system's value increases non-linearly over time as accumulated data enables deeper connections, more accurate pattern recognition, and better-calibrated AI outputs. Each new piece of information enriches the context for all future queries.

## Why It Matters
The system's value doesn't peak at setup -- it compounds. At 2 weeks it's occasionally useful; at 8 weeks it genuinely surprises; at 6 months it knows your thinking deeper than most new employees know a business.

## Key Ideas
- Week 2: catches connections maybe once every 3 sessions
- Week 4: starts catching genuinely surprising things
- Week 8: been studying your thinking for 2 months, knows recurring arguments and unresolved questions
- Month 6: vault knows business/thinking at depth requiring years of human onboarding
- Every client interaction logged makes pre-call briefs richer
- Every performance data point makes briefs more accurate
- The competitor who starts 6 months later cannot close the gap by working harder

## Tradeoffs
- Requires consistent capture to compound
- Old context may become stale and need pruning
- Token costs increase with vault size
