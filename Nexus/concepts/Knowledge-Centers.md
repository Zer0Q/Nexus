# Knowledge Centers

## Definition
Formal organizational bodies that structure, synthesize, and underpin knowledge in an accessible, expert manner. Sit at the formal end of the community spectrum, with strong governance, official sponsorship, and explicit knowledge retention.

## Why It Matters
In a volatile knowledge ecosystem with informal communities and ad hoc networks, knowledge centres provide the formal anchor — structured, governed repositories that ensure knowledge survives personnel changes and organizational shifts.

## Key Ideas
- Strong governance formality and high official sponsorship
- Governed and explicit knowledge retention and circulation
- Top-down emphasis in community management
- Mostly formal membership
- Serve as the formal endpoint of the community spectrum: networks → CoPs → knowledge centres
- Bridge between informal practice and institutional memory

## Tradeoffs
- Bureaucratic overhead can slow knowledge circulation
- Formal structures may discourage spontaneous contributions
- Risk of becoming outdated if not actively maintained
