# CoP Success Wheel

## Definition
A five-factor framework for evaluating and improving Communities of Practice, developed through research on 22 communities at the European Commission. Factors: shared vision, participation and engagement, knowledge retention and circulation, trust and confidence, and inclusive communication.

## Why It Matters
Without measurable success criteria, CoPs operate blindly — it's unclear whether they're adding value or consuming resources. The Success Wheel provides concrete facets to assess, track, and improve community health.

## Key Ideas
- **Shared vision**: core governing body, participatory decision-making, boundary-spanning practices, stakeholder mapping
- **Participation and engagement**: seamless offline/online combination, concrete deliverables improve vitality, leading by example
- **Knowledge retention and circulation**: knowledge pooling is strong, retention is shallow; needs clear role definitions and guidelines
- **Trust and confidence**: management engagement, peer familiarity, transparent membership, participatory procedures
- **Inclusive communication**: open information sharing, breaking down barriers, timely and democratic sharing drives impact

## Three Overarching Areas
1. **Co-ownership**: participatory decision-making culture and community governance
2. **Convening**: integrating offline and online interactions synchronously
3. **Collaboration and cooperation**: productivity, UX, and stakeholder engagement around vision and objectives

## Tradeoffs
- Proactive pull roles (investing, community building, learning facilitation) need more work than passive push roles
- Community management is often perceived as cumbersome and lacking resources
