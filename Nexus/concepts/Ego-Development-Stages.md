# Ego Development Stages

## Definition
Nine-stage model of psychological development (Greuter/Kopp/Singer) describing how consciousness evolves from impulsive reaction to integrated awareness. Each stage represents a qualitatively different way of experiencing reality.

## Why It Matters
Understanding your developmental stage explains why certain ideas resonate or feel threatening. Movement through stages follows predictable patterns and is the mechanism for lasting identity change.

## Key Ideas
- Stage 1 (Impulsive): No separation between impulse and action
- Stage 2 (Self-Protective): World is dangerous, look out for yourself
- Stage 3 (Conformist): You are your group; its rules feel like reality
- Stage 4 (Self-Aware): Inner life doesn't match exterior; cognitive dissonance
- Stage 5 (Conscientious): Build own principles, hold yourself accountable
- Stage 6 (Individualist): Principles shaped by context; hold them loosely
- Stage 7 (Strategist): Work with systems while aware of your involvement
- Stage 8 (Construct-Aware): All frameworks are useful fictions; map != territory
- Stage 9 (Unitive): Self/life separation dissolves; pure presence
- Most adults hover between stages 4-8
- Movement through stages follows a pattern: crisis -> integration -> new equilibrium

## Tradeoffs
- Higher stages don't mean "better person" -- different capabilities and blind spots
- Forcing stage movement before readiness causes regression
