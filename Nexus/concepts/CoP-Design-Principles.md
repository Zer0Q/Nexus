# CoP Design Principles

## Definition
Wenger's seven principles for designing effective Communities of Practice: treat the community as a living organism, maintain permeability to external ideas, support multiple participation levels, balance public and private spaces, deliver value, foster closeness and emotion, and maintain a healthy rhythm.

## Why It Matters
Without intentional design, CoPs either stagnate (no activity) or burn out (too much activity). These principles provide a framework for sustainable community growth that adapts to member needs while maintaining focus.

## Key Ideas
1. **Ease of evolution**: community grows organically based on member interests and needs
2. **Permeability**: member opinions penetrate the community; external ideas also enrich it
3. **Participation levels**: active core members spur peripheral members; outsiders can benefit without participating
4. **Public and private spaces**: balance between open discussion and private conversations
5. **Value**: community must deliver data of value to sustain member interest
6. **Closeness and emotion**: no pressure to meet objectives; motivation toward the topic creates positive climate
7. **Rhythm**: measure the pace — too slow loses interest, too fast overwhelms members

## Tradeoffs
- Too open (high permeability): risk of losing focus
- Too closed: stagnation and groupthink
- Balancing public/private requires ongoing judgment
