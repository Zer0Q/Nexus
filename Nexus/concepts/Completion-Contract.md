# Completion Contract

## Definition
A Completion Contract defines what "done" means before agent work starts, including the required outcomes and evidence that proves each requirement has been satisfied.

## Why It Matters
Agents often declare work complete when the visible path looks plausible. A completion contract turns "done" into a verifiable claim and prevents the loop from stopping on vibes.

## Key Ideas
- Write acceptance criteria before implementation begins.
- Map every criterion to evidence such as tests, screenshots, logs, docs, or review verdicts.
- Refuse to claim success when evidence is missing.
- Works as a stronger [[concepts/Stop-Condition]] for goal-driven loops.
