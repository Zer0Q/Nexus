# Operator Situational Awareness

## Definition
An operator's ability to perceive, comprehend, and project the current state of a process based on available information from HMIs, alarms, and other sources. The foundation of effective abnormal situation management.

## Why It Matters
Without situational awareness, operators cannot make timely decisions. Poor HMI design and alarm floods directly degrade situational awareness, leading to missed critical alarms, incorrect responses, and potentially industrial accidents.

## Key Ideas
- **Three levels:** perception (seeing the data), comprehension (understanding what it means), projection (anticipating future state)
- **Degraded by:** alarm floods, colorful cluttered HMIs, inconsistent navigation, lack of contextual information
- **Enhanced by:** High-Performance HMI (grayscale + color for abnormal), proper display hierarchy, information over data, embedded trends
- **48% improvement:** proper color use alone improves detection of abnormal situations before alarms trigger
- **Running by the alarms:** operator only responds to alarms without understanding root cause — a symptom of poor situational awareness
- **Future workforce challenge:** fewer, less-experienced operators managing more information

## Tradeoffs
- Situational awareness tools require upfront design investment
- Balancing detail with simplicity: too much data overwhelms, too little leaves gaps
