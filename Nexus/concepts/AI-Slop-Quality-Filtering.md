# AI Slop Quality Filtering

## Definition
The problem of AI-generated low-quality content flooding the internet, and techniques to filter, detect, and maintain quality standards in AI-assisted workflows.

## Why It Matters
As AI content generation becomes trivial, signal-to-noise ratio degrades. Quality filtering becomes a competitive advantage — good taste and curation matter more than raw output volume.

## Key Ideas
- AI slop: low-effort, AI-generated content that adds no value
- Detection: linguistic patterns, repetition, lack of specificity
- Prevention: human review, style guides, concrete examples requirement
- Hermes Agent can enforce quality gates via skills and prompts
- Curation > generation in saturated content markets

## Tradeoffs
- Over-filtering kills legitimate AI-assisted content
- Quality standards vary by domain
- Human review adds latency to workflows
