# Codify Without Flattening

## Definition
The practice of translating tacit knowledge into formats AI systems can learn from while preserving the richness, nuance, and decision-making context of expert judgment -- rather than reducing complex reasoning to oversimplified rules.

## Why It Matters
AI that only knows rules but not when to override them fails in edge cases. Preserving decision context means the AI understands not just what the rule is, but when and why a human would deviate from it.

## Key Ideas
- Not enough for AI to know a rule -- it must understand situational nuance
- Requires close collaboration between experts and AI engineers
- Tools: causal models, structured frameworks, knowledge graphs
- Example: chemical compliance -- AI must know when humans override rules due to context
- Professional services firm: captured concepts, placed relationships in knowledge graph
- Governance rules applied automatically, but edge cases flagged for human review

## Tradeoffs
- More complex than simple rule extraction
- Requires ongoing validation as context changes
- Balancing structure with flexibility
