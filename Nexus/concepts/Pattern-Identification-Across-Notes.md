# Pattern-Identification-Across-Notes

## Definition
The process of reading across all concept notes in a subject to identify emergent patterns that are not stated explicitly in any individual concept note. These patterns represent the highest level of understanding -- seeing what no single note names.

## Why It Matters
Individual concept notes capture discrete ideas. Pattern identification reveals the structure of the subject itself -- the relationships and principles that govern how concepts interact. This is what separates a student who has studied from one who has understood.

## Key Ideas
- Patterns emerge only when reading across the full set of concept notes
- Not stated in any individual note; they are properties of the collection
- Claude can identify patterns across the vault simultaneously that sequential reading cannot produce
- Pattern identification is the first step of the [[workflows/Synthesis-Session]]
- The pattern is the insight that restructures everything that comes after it

## Tradeoffs
- Requires a critical mass of concept notes before patterns become visible
- May produce abstract patterns that are hard to apply concretely
