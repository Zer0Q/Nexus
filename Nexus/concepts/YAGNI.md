# YAGNI

## Definition
YAGNI ("You Aren't Gonna Need It") is the engineering principle that a system should not add abstractions, features, or flexibility until a current requirement justifies them.

## Why It Matters
AI-generated code often imports patterns from large codebases and adds layers that look professional but do not serve the task. YAGNI keeps generated systems small enough to review, debug, and maintain.

## Key Ideas
- Build for the requirement in front of you.
- Treat speculative abstraction as carrying cost.
- Add flexibility when the change pressure is real.
- Use YAGNI during review to challenge AI-generated service layers, wrappers, and configuration surfaces.
