# Shared Leadership

## Definition
A flat participation model where all members teach through their experience and learn from others' — no top-down hierarchy. A coordinator may exist to connect members and organize events, but doesn't dictate knowledge flow.

## Why It Matters
Traditional knowledge transfer assumes a leader/expert who shares while others receive passively. Shared leadership flips this: every participant is both teacher and learner, creating richer knowledge exchange and stronger community bonds.

## Key Ideas
- Homogeneous participants: everyone in the same position, equal standing
- Coordinator role: connects members, suggests debate topics, promotes active participation
- Reciprocity: members contribute and benefit; one-way takers are peripheral
- Emergent expertise: reference members emerge naturally through quality of contributions, not appointment

## Tradeoffs
- Can be slower than top-down decision-making
- Requires active facilitation to ensure equal participation
- Risk of dominant voices overshadowing quieter members
