# Expected Revenue Stamp

## Definition
A metadata tag appended to output notes that estimates the revenue potential of the note type and topic. After weeks of tracking, it reveals which captures produce the highest-value output, creating a feedback loop that tells the vault owner what to capture more of.

## Why It Matters
Without revenue tracking, you cannot optimize your capture strategy. The stamp turns the vault into a self-improving system: it learns which topics and note types are most profitable and signals you to capture more of those. It is the data layer behind shipping discipline.

## Key Ideas
- Appended at the bottom of every output generated by the Output Generator prompt
- Tags each output with an estimated revenue range based on the note type and topic
- After a few weeks, aggregate data shows which note types and topics produce highest-value output
- Creates a feedback loop: capture more of what earns more
- The vault starts telling you what to capture, not just what to think about

## Tradeoffs
- Revenue estimates are inherently speculative in early stages
- Requires manual validation of actual revenue vs expected revenue to calibrate
- Adds metadata overhead to every output note
