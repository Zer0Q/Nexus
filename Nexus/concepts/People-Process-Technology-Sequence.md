# People Process Technology Sequence

## Definition
The correct order for AI transformation: people first (they understand the real work), process second (automation only scales when the process is stable and owned), technology third (AI as the enabler, not the starting point).

## Why It Matters
Most AI projects start with technology — buying tools, building models, deploying automation. This reverses the causal chain: without engaged people and stable processes, technology amplifies existing weaknesses. Getting the sequence wrong is the most common reason AI transformations stall.

## Key Ideas
- **People first:** they understand the real work; involve them from day one so they feel ownership rather than imposition
- **Process second:** automation only scales when the process underneath is stable, clear, and owned
- **Technology third:** AI should be the enabler, not the starting point
- **Oxytocin (belonging):** released when people feel part of a group working toward something together — lowers stress and defensiveness that kill change programs
- **Dopamine (progress):** released when hitting goals or clearing milestones — visible, repeated proof that effort is working
- **Belonging makes people willing to start; progress makes them want to continue**
- **Transformation that ignores both chemicals feels like something being done to people**
- **AI's first role:** visual management — making process problems visible before automating them

## Tradeoffs
- People-first approach takes longer initially but prevents costly rework
- Stakeholders may pressure for quick tech wins before process is ready
