# Harnessability

## Definition
Harnessability is how easily a codebase or workflow can be regulated by agent guides, deterministic checks, feedback sensors, and self-correction loops.

## Why It Matters
Some systems are naturally easier for coding agents to work in because they expose strong types, clear module boundaries, fast tests, architecture rules, and reliable tooling. Low-harnessability systems need more human supervision even when the model is strong.

## Key Ideas
- Strong types, explicit boundaries, and fast tests increase harnessability.
- Legacy systems often need harnesses most but make them hardest to build.
- Greenfield teams can choose stacks and templates partly by available harness controls.
- Harnessability should be considered an architectural quality.
