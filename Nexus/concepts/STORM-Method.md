## Definition

STORM (Synthesis of Topic Outlines through Retrieval and Multi-perspective Question Asking) is a research method published at NAACL 2024 by Stanford OVAL Lab. It produces articles that are 25% more organized and 10% broader in coverage than single-perspective methods.

## The 4-Prompt Workflow

1. **Multi-Perspective Scan:** Simulate 5 expert perspectives on the topic
2. **Contradiction Map:** Map where perspectives disagree and agree
3. **Synthesis:** Pull everything into a research briefing
4. **Peer Review:** Self-critique the synthesis

## Key Insight

Multi-perspective questioning catches blind spots that single-prompt research never sees. The contradictions between perspectives are where real understanding lives.

## Implementation

The method can be run inside any LLM (Claude, etc.) with 4 copy-paste prompts in ~5 minutes. No software or setup required. The Stanford code is open source (MIT license) at github.com/stanford-oval/storm.
