# Public Healthcare AI Investment

## Definition
The argument that public healthcare systems should invest heavily in AI model access for medical research and patient care. Top models are expensive and paywalled, creating an equity gap where only those who can afford API costs benefit from AI-assisted medical analysis.

## Why It Matters
The ARROWHEAD methodology requires access to ChatGPT 5 Pro and Claude Opus 4.8 MAX -- both expensive paid services. If AI-assisted medical research produces genuinely better outcomes, restricting access to those who can pay is a public health problem.

## Key Ideas
- "Public Healthcare should invest a lot (but A LOT) in this"
- The cost barrier limits access to AI-assisted medical research
- Public investment would democratize access to better medical analysis
- The methodology is replicable -- the barrier is model access, not technical complexity
- Future prediction: medical histories will be fully digitized and "pre-chewed" for LLM consumption

## Tradeoffs
- Public investment in AI infrastructure is politically challenging
- Model costs may not decline fast enough to make this feasible
- Liability and governance frameworks need to precede widespread adoption
- Risk of over-reliance on AI in clinical settings
