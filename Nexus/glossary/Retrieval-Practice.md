# Retrieval Practice

## Definition
The umbrella term for any learning activity that requires pulling information from memory rather than receiving it passively. Writing a concept in your own words, answering a test question, and explaining a concept to someone else are all forms of retrieval practice.

## Key Properties
- Creates stronger and more durable memory traces than passive review
- Improves both long-term retention and applicability of knowledge
- The cognitive effort of translating from source language to your own language is the first act of retrieval practice
- The Obsidian-Claude learning system is essentially a systematic retrieval practice machine
