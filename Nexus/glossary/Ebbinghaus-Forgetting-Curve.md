# Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve

## Definition
Hermann Ebbinghaus's 1885 discovery that memory retention follows a predictable decay curve: roughly 70% of new information is forgotten within 24 hours unless reviewed at strategic intervals. The curve shows that review timing matters more than review frequency.

## Key Properties
- Retention drops sharply in the first 24 hours
- Each successful review flattens the curve, extending the interval before the next review
- Reviews timed at the point of near-forgetfulness produce the strongest memory traces
- The curve is the scientific basis for [[Spaced-Repetition-System]]
