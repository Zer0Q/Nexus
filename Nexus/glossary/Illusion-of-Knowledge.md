# Illusion of Knowledge

## Definition
The false sense of familiarity that results from passive review of study material. Re-reading notes or highlighting textbooks creates the feeling of understanding without producing the ability to retrieve and apply the information.

## Key Properties
- Familiarity is not the same as being able to use knowledge
- The illusion is strongest immediately after re-reading, when the material feels most known
- Active recall exposes the illusion by revealing what cannot be retrieved
- The most popular study methods (re-reading, highlighting, cramming) are also the least effective
